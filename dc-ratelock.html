How we know

How we know your number is real

Your number isn't a salesperson's guess. It's built from data anyone can check. Here's exactly where it comes from.

Google Solar API

Google maps the sun that hits your roof across the year, because the same roof gets different sun on different slopes. That tells us how much power your roof can actually make.

Nearmap aerial imagery

We read your actual roof from high-resolution aerial photos, because your roof's size, shape and shading decide what fits. No stock photo. Your roof.

Live Pepco rates

We run your number against what Pepco charges right now, because the saving is the gap between their price and yours. Today's rate, not last year's.

US Energy Information Administration

We check our numbers against federal energy data (EIA Form 861), because public data keeps us honest. If the figures don't line up, we don't show them.

Put simply. Real roof from the imagery. Real sun from Google. Real price from Pepco. Checked against federal data. That's your number.

Why now

Why looking now actually matters

Pepco rates keep climbing

Pepco raised DC rates 17.7% last year. Then filed to raise them again. Every month you rent power, you rent it at a price built to climb. That's not a sales line. It's the rate filings.

The solar tax credit rules changed

The federal solar tax credit rules changed this year. In plain terms. From January 2026, homeowners who buy panels outright no longer get the federal credit. One path still carries it. When a third-party owner holds the system, the credit can still apply, and that's the kind of plan SolarSight offers. It's worth knowing where you stand before you decide.

Picture next summer. The DC heat is the same. Pepco's rate is higher again. But your bill is locked, lower, and it doesn't scare you anymore. That's the whole point of seeing your number now. So you're set before the next hike, not after it.
