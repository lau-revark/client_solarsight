Privacy Policy

How SolarSight collects, uses, and protects your information — including email and SMS communications.

Effective date: 13 April 2026  •  Last updated: 13 April 2026

1. Introduction

Phoenix Solar Group Inc. ("Company," "we," "us," or "our") operates SolarSight, a commercial solar site assessment and prospecting platform (the "Service"). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our Service, including our website, mobile applications, SMS/text messaging communications, and any related services.

By accessing or using SolarSight, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to be bound by this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with the terms of this Privacy Policy, please do not access or use the Service.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide

We collect information you voluntarily provide when you register for an account, subscribe to our services, request a solar estimate, or contact us. This may include: your name, email address, mobile phone number, company name, job title, billing and payment information, and any other information you choose to provide.

2.2 Property and Geographic Data

SolarSight is designed to assess commercial properties for solar potential. In connection with this Service, we collect and process: property addresses and geographic coordinates, building footprint and roof segment data, parcel boundary information, publicly available property ownership records, satellite and aerial imagery data, and building characteristics (size, orientation, shading factors).

2.3 Third-Party Data Sources

We aggregate data from various third-party sources to provide our Service, including: Google Solar API for building insights and solar analysis, Nearmap for high-resolution aerial imagery, NREL (National Renewable Energy Laboratory) for weather and production modeling data, utility rate databases for electricity pricing information, public property records and parcel data systems, and geocoding services for address verification.

2.4 Automatically Collected Information

When you access our Service, we automatically collect certain information, including: device information (browser type, operating system, device identifiers), IP address and approximate location, usage data (pages viewed, features used, search queries), cookies and similar tracking technologies, and log data (access times, duration of visits, referring URLs).

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to: provide, maintain, and improve our Service; generate solar site assessments, feasibility analyses, and financial projections; facilitate property prospecting and owner identification; process transactions and send related information; send administrative communications, service updates, and SMS verification codes; respond to your inquiries and provide customer support; analyze usage patterns to enhance user experience; detect, prevent, and address technical issues or fraudulent activity; comply with legal obligations; and enforce our terms, conditions, and policies.

4. SMS / Text Messaging Communications

4.1 Types of Messages

When you provide your mobile phone number and opt in, we may send you SMS text messages for the following purposes:

  • Account verification and authentication — one-time passcodes (OTPs) to verify your identity and secure access to your account.

  • Transactional notifications — confirmations, status updates, and service alerts relating to your account or requested estimates.

  • Customer service — responses to inquiries you have initiated.

SolarSight does not send marketing or promotional SMS messages.

4.2 Consent

You must expressly opt in to receive SMS messages from us by entering your mobile number in the SolarSight estimate or account registration flow and affirmatively confirming that you agree to receive text messages. By providing your mobile number and opting in, you consent to receive SMS messages from Phoenix Solar Group Inc. at the number provided.

4.3 Message Frequency, Rates, and Carrier Disclaimer

Message frequency varies based on your use of the Service. Message and data rates may apply. We are not responsible for any charges imposed by your mobile carrier. Message delivery is subject to the availability of your mobile carrier's network, and we do not guarantee delivery of every message.

4.4 Opt-Out / HELP

You may opt out of SMS messages at any time by replying STOP to any message you receive from us. After you send STOP, we will send a confirmation message, after which you will no longer receive SMS messages from us (except as required to complete transactions you have initiated). For help, reply HELP or contact us using the details in Section 12.

4.5 No Sharing of Mobile Information for Marketing

We do not sell, rent, lease, or share mobile phone numbers, SMS opt-in data, or SMS consent information with any third parties or affiliates for their marketing or promotional purposes. Mobile information is shared only with service providers that support the delivery of our SMS messages (such as our messaging platform provider, Twilio Inc.) and only to the extent necessary to deliver the messages you have requested. This restriction applies to all categories of mobile information collected through the Service, including phone numbers and opt-in status, and this restriction is not waived by any other sharing disclosure elsewhere in this Privacy Policy.

4.6 Supported Carriers

SMS messages are sent via the Twilio messaging platform and are supported on all major US wireless carriers, including AT&T, Verizon, T-Mobile, US Cellular, and their prepaid/MVNO brands. Carriers are not liable for delayed or undelivered messages.

5. Disclosure of Your Information

We may share your information in the following circumstances:

Service Providers. We may share information with third-party vendors, consultants, and service providers who perform services on our behalf, including payment processing, data analysis, email delivery, SMS/messaging delivery (Twilio Inc.), hosting services, and customer support. These providers are contractually restricted from using your information for their own purposes.

Business Transfers. In connection with any merger, acquisition, sale of assets, or bankruptcy, your information may be transferred to a successor entity.

Legal Requirements. We may disclose information when required by law, regulation, legal process, or governmental request, or when we believe disclosure is necessary to protect our rights, your safety, or the safety of others.

With Your Consent. We may share your information with third parties when you have given us explicit consent to do so.

Aggregated Data. We may share aggregated or de-identified information that cannot reasonably be used to identify you.

Mobile information exclusion. As stated in Section 4.5, mobile phone numbers and SMS opt-in data are expressly excluded from the categories of information that may be shared with third parties for marketing or promotional purposes under any of the circumstances described above.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, including to satisfy legal, accounting, or reporting requirements. Property assessment data and reports may be retained for historical reference and to improve our analytical models. SMS opt-in records (including the timestamp and source of consent) are retained for the duration of your opt-in status and for a minimum of three (3) years after opt-out, as required for compliance audit purposes. When you terminate your account, we will delete or anonymize your personal information within 90 days, unless retention is required by law or legitimate business purposes.

7. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures designed to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include encryption of data in transit and at rest, access controls and authentication requirements, regular security assessments, and employee training on data protection. However, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

8. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information, including the right to: access the personal information we hold about you; correct inaccurate or incomplete information; request deletion of your personal information; object to or restrict certain processing activities; data portability (receiving your data in a portable format); and withdraw consent where processing is based on consent (including SMS consent, which may be withdrawn at any time by replying STOP to any SMS message). To exercise these rights, please contact us using the information provided in Section 12.

9. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to collect information about your browsing activities. You can manage your cookie preferences through your browser settings. Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our Service.

10. Third-Party Links and Services

Our Service may contain links to third-party websites or integrate with third-party services. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these third parties. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party sites or services you access.

11. Children's Privacy

Our Service is not directed to individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that we have collected personal information from a child, we will take steps to delete such information promptly.

If you are a California resident, you may have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), including the right to know what personal information we collect, the right to delete personal information, the right to opt-out of the sale or sharing of personal information, and the right to non-discrimination for exercising your privacy rights. To exercise these rights, please contact us using the information in Section 12.

Our Service is primarily operated in the United States. If you are accessing our Service from outside the United States, please be aware that your information may be transferred to, stored, and processed in the United States where our servers are located. By using our Service, you consent to the transfer of your information to the United States and acknowledge that data protection laws in the United States may differ from those in your country of residence.
