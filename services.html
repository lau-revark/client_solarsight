The Pre-Qualification Report

What's in your Solar Pre-Qualification Report

A detailed breakdown of what SolarSight calculates for your home — and what it doesn't commit you to.

Report Contents

Five things your report includes

1

Estimated Annual Savings (in dollars)

The dollar figure your home is estimated to save each year on electricity based on your local utility rate and your roof's solar production capacity. This is a specific number — not a range or a percentage.

2

Roof Suitability Assessment

An assessment of your roof's orientation, tilt angle, usable area, and shading conditions — derived from Google Solar API and Nearmap imagery data.

3

Estimated System Size

The approximate solar system size (in kilowatts) that your roof can accommodate and that would optimise your savings potential.

4

Available Incentives Overview (DC/MD)

A summary of relevant solar incentive programs available in DC and Maryland, including the federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC). Note: incentive details are provided for information only — amounts depend on individual circumstances and regulatory conditions at the time of installation.

5

Next Steps (if you want to proceed)

If your pre-qualification looks promising and you'd like to explore further, the report outlines how to take the next step — on your terms, in your own time.

Clarity matters

What Pre-Qual is NOT

We believe in being clear about what you're getting — and what you're not.

✗

Not a binding quote

Your pre-qualification report does not constitute a quote, proposal, or offer from SolarSight or Phoenix Solar Group.

✗

Not a system design

A full system design requires a qualified installer to conduct a site inspection. The pre-qualification report is a data-based estimate, not a technical design document.

✗

Not a commitment

Receiving your pre-qualification report creates no obligation on your part — or ours. No sales call will be scheduled automatically.
